Jon Lee Steak Hoagie

The Jon Lee Steak Hoagie was born before my time, but I was fortunate enough to come along in an appropriate window of opportunity.

This sandwich was a favorite off menu item of the employees of Attractions restaurant, utilized to shake up the monotony of on menu items or as a mild to moderate hangover cure.

The sandwich gets its name from its creator, Attractions kitchen manager Jon Lee.

This divine creation takes a traditional beef hoagie patty and incorporates intense flavors from all directions, including buffalo sauce, cajun mayo, garlic herb butter, and two kinds of cheese.

Ingredients

  • 1 beef hoagie patty
  • 1 steak hoagie roll
  • 1/2 cup buffalo sauce
  • 1/4 cup mayo
  • 2 tsp cajun seasoning
  • 1/2 cup shredded iceberg lettuce
  • 2 slices of cheddar cheese
  • 2 slices of provolone cheese
  • garlic herb butter for brushing the hoagie roll

Steps

  1. Lightly toast hoagie roll in toaster or over, set aside
  2. Combine mayo and cajun seasoning, stir, set aside
  3. Place hoagie patty on hot grill for 2-3 minutes
  4. Flip hoagie patty, and brush generously with buffalo sauce
  5. After 1-2 minutes, flip patty again, and brush generously with buffalo sauce
  6. While patty continues to cook, brush both top and bottom pieces of roll with garlic herb butter
  7. Place roll pieces directly on grill
  8. Place the four slices of cheese on the upward facing top section of the roll, alternating cheddar and provolone across the length of the roll
  9. Grill until a golden brown crusts forms on the grill facing roll pieces (this may happen very quickly, and it is easy to burn, so please pay close attention)
  10. Remove roll pieces and set aside, cheese will continue to melt
  11. Flip hoagie patty two additional times, brushing with buffalo sauce each time, letting each side briefly grill for 20-30 seconds
  12. Spread cajun seasoning and mayo mixture on the ungrilled bottom section of the roll
  13. Add shredded lettuce on top of mayo spread
  14. Remove patty from grill and place on top of shredded lettuce
  15. Place top section of hoagie roll on top of patty
  16. Cut in half, grab extra napkins, sit down, take a deep breath
  17. Enjoy this marvelous creation, goes well with a side of fries
